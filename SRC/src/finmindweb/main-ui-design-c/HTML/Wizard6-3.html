Coverage time.

Contrast to term life insurance, permanent life insurance provides protection throughout the insured's life. All types of permanent life insurance have cash value that the owner of the policy can access as long as certain criteria are met.
Whole Life: provides guaranteed protection for one's entire life with fixed growth rate at around 3% annually, in general with all insurance companies. The premiums are predictable and stable, payable throughout the lifetime of the policy, up to age 98.
Universal Life: offers more flexibility in terms of death benefit amounts and premium payments. Flexible premiums allow you to adjust payments, or to choose a fixed, consistent premium amount. As life changes, it allows for adjustment of benefit amount. The cash value grows at interest rates on par with bank savings.
Indexed Universal Life: is the most recent innovation in the insurance industry, offered by a limited group of life insurance companies in the world that dominate the marketplace. On top of the flexibility that is offered by all universal life policies, the cash value grows at a rate referring to one or more indexes, such as the S&P 500 infex alone, or in combination with Euro Stoxx 50 index, Hang Seng Index, Nasdaq-100. It offers potential cash accumulation up to the cap rate offered by a specific insurance company, at 10% or 12%. In the meanwhile, the risk of market volatility is eliminated to have the interest floor at zero.
Variable Universal Life: offers great cash account growth possibility, and the risk of substantial loss, with the exposure to market fluctuation. It allows flexibility in death benefit and premium payments, but no guarantee should equity market be in a sustained losses situation.
